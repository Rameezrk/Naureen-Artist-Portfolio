Naureen Khawaja Hafeez is a Bradford based illustrator and digital artist who designs work for publishing, film, theatre and online platforms.

Her work is inspired by her Pakistani culture and illuminates intricate detail, vivid colours and layers.

In 2018, she began working with the National Science and Media Museum to create work for their website and a variety of exhibitions exploring identity and the artistic process.

Naureen designed the first cover for Pennycress Magazine (https://pennycress.bigcartel.com/). Issue #1 ‘So, where are you from?’ championed creatives of colour in the North of England. She has also designed Issue #2 ‘What would aunty do’ discussing creatives of colour exploring cultural tradition and family legacy in the North of England.

In 2019, she was commissioned to create storyboards for ‘The Ancestors', a short film by Zodwa Nyoni about Zimbabwean rituals and belonging. It’s produced by BBC Films, BFI Film Hub North and Redbag Pictures.

The same year, she created five different postcards to accompany Afshan D'souza- Lodhi's debut poetry collection [re: desire]. The collection explores the yearning to love, be loved and belong from a desi (South Asian) perspective. Naureen's work expanded on the imagery of how to eat a mango properly.

As well as working as a creative, Naureen holds a Degree in Creative Writing and is qualified in advanced SEN and classroom behaviour management. Her work in primary and high schools supporting children with a variety of special needs has taught her the important to finding resources that are accessible and celebrate neurodiversity.

Naureen is currently working on a graphic novel set in ancient India.
